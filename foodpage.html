There are tons of foods you can try in Penang.

Penang offers a rich variety of delicious street foods, blending Chinese, Malay, and Indian culinary traditions.

1. Char Kway Teow

Char Kway Teow is a popular Malaysian dish featuring stir-fried flat noodles with soy sauce, prawns, cockles, egg, Chinese sausage, and bean sprouts, known for its smoky flavor.

Recommended place:

Perak Road Night Char Koay Teow

Presgrave Street Corner Heng Kee Char Koay Teow

2. Asam Laksa

Asam Laksa is a spicy and sour noodle soup made with mackerel, tamarind, and fresh vegetables.

Recommended place:

Laksa Mamu Famous Sungai Pinang

Mollie Dessert House and Asam Laksa

3. Hokkien Mee

Hokkien Mee is a rich and flavorful noodle soup originating from Penang, made with a combination of egg and rice noodles, prawns, pork slices, hard-boiled eggs, and bean sprouts, all served in a savory broth.

Recommended place:

Famous Penang Hokkien Mee

Wufoo Hokkien Prawn Noodle·Lor Mee

4. Nasi Kandar

Nasi Kandar is a beloved Malaysian dish consisting of steamed rice served with an array of rich and flavorful curries, meats, and vegetables, often accompanied by pickles and fried items.

Recommended place:

Deens Maju Nasi Kandar

Restoran Nasi Kandar Bathusa

5. Penang Rojak

Penang Rojak is a tangy fruit and vegetable salad with a spicy shrimp paste dressing and crushed peanuts.

Recommended place:

Rojak Candu Sg

Ang Meng Hooi Rojak Cucuk

6. Cendol

Cendol is a sweet dessert with green rice flour jelly, coconut milk, and palm sugar syrup.

Recommended place:

Penang Road Famous Teochew Cendol

7. Loh Bak

Loh Bak is a popular Penang snack consisting of marinated pork rolls wrapped in bean curd skin, then deep-fried until crispy. Served with a flavorful dipping sauce, it’s a savory and satisfying treat.

Recommended place:

Special Loh Bak

Tan Jetty Prawn Fritters & Loh Bak

8. Oyster Omelette

Oyster Omelette, or Oh Chien, is a savory dish featuring a fluffy omelette with fresh oysters, egg, and a crispy texture, often served with a flavorful chili sauce.

Recommended place:

Ah Soon Fried Oyster

Bayan Baru Fried OysterBayan Baru Fried Oyster

9. Mee goreng

Mee Goreng is a spicy stir-fried noodle dish made with yellow noodles, prawns, chicken, tofu, and vegetables, flavored with a savory blend of sauces and spices.

Recommended place:

Mee Goreng Warisan

Hameed Pata Mee Sotong

10. Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is a traditional Malaysian dish featuring fragrant rice cooked in coconut milk, served with sambal, crispy anchovies, peanuts, cucumber, and often a boiled egg.

Recommended place:

Nasi Lemak Ciksue

Nasi Lemak Baloot
